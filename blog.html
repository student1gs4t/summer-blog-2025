June 15, 2025 – Summer Begins!

Summer has officially started, and I’m beyond excited! No more alarms, no more homework—just weeks of freedom and fun. I kicked off the day with my usual ritual: waking up late and eating a ridiculously large breakfast (pancakes and strawberries, in case you were wondering).

I spent the afternoon brainstorming what I want to do this summer. My list is already longer than I can probably manage—learning to skateboard, going on hikes, testing new ice cream flavors, and maybe even starting a YouTube channel? Who knows. For now, I’m just enjoying the feeling of summer stretching out in front of me.

Tonight, my family and I are going for a sunset walk around the lake, and I can’t wait. There’s something about warm summer nights that makes everything feel magical. Here’s to a summer full of adventures!

June 28, 2025 – First Big Adventure!

I finally checked off my first summer adventure: hiking to the top of Eagle Rock! It was way harder than I expected, but totally worth it. The view at the top was breathtaking—rolling green hills, a sparkling river, and nothing but sky all around.

My legs were basically jelly by the time I reached the summit, but I felt so proud of myself. My friends and I had packed sandwiches, and let me just say, food tastes ten times better when you’ve earned it. We sat there for an hour, just enjoying the view and joking around.

On the way down, we found a hidden waterfall! It was freezing, but that didn’t stop us from splashing around. I’ll definitely be sore tomorrow, but this was exactly the kind of adventure I wanted for my summer.

July 10, 2025 – Rainy Day Fun

The weather took a turn today, and instead of sunshine, we got endless rain. Normally, I’d be bummed, but honestly, it was kind of nice. I spent the day curled up with a book, drinking hot cocoa (yes, in July!), and listening to the sound of the rain hitting the windows.

Since going outside was a no-go, I finally started working on my summer scrapbook. I’ve collected little things from the past few weeks—ticket stubs, pressed flowers, and a million photos—and now it’s all coming together into something really cool.

Even though I didn’t go anywhere today, I realized that summer isn’t just about big adventures. It’s also about slowing down and appreciating the little things.

July 22, 2025 – Ice Cream Taste Test!

Today was officially Ice Cream Challenge Day, and it was AMAZING. My best friend and I decided to test the weirdest flavors we could find. We hit three different ice cream shops, trying everything from lavender honey to spicy mango.

Some flavors were incredible—like the toasted marshmallow one that tasted exactly like a campfire. Others… not so much. Let’s just say pickle ice cream should not exist. Who even thought that was a good idea?!

By the end of the day, I was completely stuffed but ridiculously happy. I think my favorite part was laughing so hard we couldn’t breathe every time we tried a terrible flavor. 10/10, would do again!

August 5, 2025 – Backyard Camping!

Since we couldn’t go on an actual camping trip, my friends and I decided to set up camp in my backyard. We pitched a tent, made a fire (well, a fire pit), and even attempted roasting marshmallows over candles when the fire kept going out.

We spent hours telling ghost stories, playing card games, and staring up at the stars. The best part? Falling asleep to the sound of crickets and waking up when the sun started peeking through the tent.

It might not have been real camping, but honestly? It felt just as magical. Maybe next time, we’ll actually make it to the woods!

August 21, 2025 – The Final Days of Summer

How is summer almost over already? It feels like just yesterday I was making my summer bucket list, and now I’m trying to check off the last few things before school starts again.

I spent today biking around the neighborhood, taking in the last golden afternoons of freedom. My friends and I stopped by our favorite park, and we just sat there, reminiscing about all the best moments from the past two months.

This summer has been everything I wanted it to be—adventurous, relaxing, chaotic, and fun. I’m definitely sad it’s ending, but I know I’ll carry these memories with me into the new school year.
